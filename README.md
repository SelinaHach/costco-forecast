# Costco Wholesale (COST) — Driver-Based Forecast Model

https://selinahach.github.io/costco-forecast/

FP&A Portfolio Project | FY2023A – FY2027E

## What This Project Does

Built a 3-year financial forecast model for Costco to answer: **what actually drives Costco's profit, and what should leadership watch closest?**

## What I Found

Costco sells merchandise at almost no profit — merchandise margin is 11.12%, minus SG&A at 9.25%, leaving only ~1.87% on every dollar sold. The business only works because membership fees (5.3B) drop in at near-100% margin, making up ~51% of operating income.

So membership is the foundation — without it, Costco's business model doesn't work. But membership is stable (89.8% renewal, grows steadily every year). It's not what keeps the CFO up at night.

What keeps the CFO up at night is **margin**. Because that ~1.87% spread sits on top of 270B in net sales, a tiny shift moves profit dramatically:

- ±50bps in merchandise margin = ±1,170mm Net Income (12.5% of profit)
- ±50bps in SG&A = ±1,170mm Net Income (12.5% of profit)
- ±1pp in revenue growth = only ±43mm (0.5% of profit)

Revenue growth barely matters for profit. Margin is 27x more impactful.

## Recommendations

1. **Monitor margin like a hawk** — tariffs, commodity costs, Kirkland Signature mix. This is the biggest swing factor.
2. **Control SG&A** — wages rising deliberately (avg ~32/hr). Continue investing in people but offset with automation.
3. **Protect membership quality** — renewal rate dropped 70bps due to digital sign-ups. Membership is the heart of the business. Don't take it for granted.
